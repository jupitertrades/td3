The Inflections

What key inflections take us to a world in which computer-assisted journalism is increasingly prevalent?
Technology
Data is everywhere, and certain data sets are increasingly open (i.e. OpenData). Opportunity shifts to applications like automated journalism that can leverage this as an input.
Belief
The rise of GPT-3 has at least planted the idea that automated content can happen in a useful manner. Fact-checking utilized by the major social media players is further supporting widespread belief that computers have a role to play in determining facts.

Plausible Futures

Where will these inflections take us? 
Robo journalists: creating content readers want to read but writers don't want to write.
Jeremy Gilbert, director of strategic initiatives at The Washington Post, said Heliograf was developed as a tool to help the newspaper’s editorial team.“The Post has an incredible team of reporters and editors and we didn’t want to replace them,” Gilbert told AFP. Gilbert said the bot can deliver and update stories more quickly as they develop, allowing reporters to concentrate on other tasks, and that reaction has been generally positive.“The surprise was that a lot of people came up and said, ‘I do this story every week; is this something we can automate?'” Gilbert said.“These weren’t stories that anyone wanted to do.” - Grendz

Insights

What are the secrets of riding the inflections to get to the future?
  • Machine generated media can produce large quantities of media about every conceivable topic. This facilitates superior personalization of media.
  • Machine generated media can be rooted in data, which facilitates data-driven journalism -- and thus may be less susceptible to the problems found in clickbait journalism.
  • Because of the cost advantages introduced by machine generated media, investigative journalism becomes more economically viable
