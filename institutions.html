Market Capitalization Number of shares outstanding multiplied by the close price. Rolf W. Banz

Sector Price vs Sector 100 SMA The average percent change in the market capitalization of tickers in the sector the given ticker is in relative to the 100 day simple moving average of the market capitalization of the given sector. In sum, an assessment of how the sector as a whole the ticker is in is trending relative to its 100 day simple moving average.
Sector Price vs Sector 200 SMA The average percent change in the market capitalization of tickers in the sector the given ticker is in relative to the 200 day simple moving average of the market capitalization of the given sector. In sum, an assessment of how the sector as a whole the ticker is in is trending relative to its 200 day simple moving average. Gary Antonacci
Ticker 100 SMA vs Sector 100 SMA The percent change in the ticker's market capitalization relative to its 100 day simple moving average divided by the percent change in the market capitalization of the ticker relative to the 100 day simple moving average of the sector's market capitalization. Basically, a summary of how the ticker has performed relative to its sector performance over the past 100 days.
Ticker 200 SMA vs Sector 200 SMA The percent change in the ticker's market capitalization relative to its 200 day simple moving average divided by the percent change in the market capitalization of the ticker relative to the 200 day simple moving average of the sector's market capitalization. Basically, a summary of how the ticker has performed relative to its sector performance over the past 200 days. David Ryan
Dilution Factor Number of fully diluted shares the ticker has divided by the current number of shares issued.

Fine Print

Ticker/date combinations in which there are less than 200 consecutive preceding trading days for which the ticker has been listed on a US exchange, or which have not yet reported at least two quarters worth of earnings and revenue, are excluded from the data set. Moreover, in some cases, some of the data for certain ticker/date combinations has been imputed, and is not directly provided by financial statements. For definitions on when imputation has occurred, please see the complete Model One Data Dictionary.
